    5 Insider Tips for Going Freelance

    Jul 27, 2015
    • 7,539 views
    • 119 Likes
    • 42 Comments
    • Share on LinkedIn
    • Share on Facebook
    • Share on Twitter

    As someone who’s worked within the creative industry for more than 5 years, and who has recently entered the fast paced world of freelancing, I can safely say that I've learned a lot in a short space of time.

    So if you're thinking about taking the leap into freelancing then here are some undoubtedly handy tips to help get you started.

    1. Organise 

    “Fail to plan, plan to fail” - Winston Churchill

    The first thing you should do when you begin freelancing is get organised! One of the cheapest and most useful items I have ever bought to date is a pin board - which today is home to my typed out list of short, medium and long term goals.

    My Freelance Pro Tip: Type up your own list of goals and print them out; we are extremely visual beings so to really drill it home you need to physically see your goals every day to keep you focused and on track.

    2. Portfolio

    “The life of a designer is a life of fight. Fight against the ugliness.” - Massimo Vignelli

    Now that you're organised it's time to make sure that your portfolio is up to scratch.

    This applies to all creative professions: be it design, photography, musical or anything in between. Ideally you should aim to have around 8 - 12 completed projects. However the main thing you need to focus on is the quality of your work, it's better to have fewer projects of exceptional quality than lots of poor/average quality work.

    Also, if a potential client visits your website the likelihood of them looking at every one of your projects is very slim so make sure that every piece of work is to a high standard.

    My Freelance Pro Tip: It pays to tell the story of each project so that potential clients gain an understanding of the ways in which you work, and what you can do for them. So why not add a project overview, including the client’s brief and any challenges that you had to overcome. Finish off each project page with a testimonial from the happy client.

    3. Social

    "If you build it, Like it, Pin it, Share it, Tweet it, They will Come!" - Unknown

    As a freelancer, you need to tell people why you’re awesome. After all you no longer have a marketing team to do this for you, or a management team above you who will secure you a consistent work flow and that all important income.

    So as soon as you have your portfolio together ­it's time to shout about your work, sign up to lots of social networking sites:

    • Linkedin
    • Instagram
    • Twitter
    • Behance
    • YouTube
    • Dribbble
    • Vimeo
    • Hiive

    Along with any other social networking websites you can find in your chosen field! Fill in every part of your profile page. Don't just assume people are going to click through all of the websites you've signed up to, so ­keep your work and details up to date and consistent across all sites.

    My Freelance Pro Tip: Use these social networking sites to provide advice that both demonstrates you as an expert within your field, whilst maintaining just enough knowledge for clients to want and need your services.

    4. Contacts

    "It's not about who you know, it's about who knows you" - Unknown

    Now you have the portfolio and social media profiles, it’s time to begin adding people, talking to people and posting out relevant content.

    My Freelance Pro Tip: After a month or so of doing this you will see which social media websites work for you then focus on promoting yourself on those sites. You should also keep a record of all of your social media activity (or use a tool that does this for you, such as Hootsuite) and never stop assessing what forms of content are truly engaging with people and growing your fan base.

    5. Work

    “Nothing in the world is worth having or worth doing unless it means effort…” - Theodore Roosevelt

    Now that you're equipped with a high quality portfolio and a strong contact base it's time to work... and work hard!­ Post your work in progress of new projects on your social media sites to gain more followers and interest, also don't forget to utilise hashtags for others to find your work!

    My Freelance Pro Tip: Getting started within the freelance world requires hard work, and plenty of it, often at discounted prices until both your reputation and client base build. Due to the workloads that this can produce it can be easy to get bogged down with the relentless undertaking of projects and sometimes lose sight of their long-term targets and aspirations. So be sure to continually reassess your goals, and never stop developing better work ethics and techniques in order to work smarter.

    I’d lastly finish just by saying that making the switch can be scary, but being informed, connected and dedicated to making it work really is all that you need to be your own boss.

    Thanks for Reading!
