Getting Paid 101: Quick Tips for Avoiding Late Payments and Dealing with Non-Payers

Join 10,992 other freelancers enjoying 7 free lessons and weekly articles. Get your free lessons and articles here.

Written by Julie Elster

As a freelancer and AR specialist I fully understand the importance of not only being an expert at your craft, but keeping up on those client payments. It is possible to send your clients’ invoices that they will want to pay, and pay on time! Let’s start with some quick tips for getting paid.

Know Your Value and Know Your Client

You want to attract the right client. There are two ways of doing this. First, know the value you provide and charge rates that reflect that value. And second you need to qualify every client that you work with.

As a freelancer it might seem that the best way to attract business and keep the clients coming in would be to lower your rates. You might think this gives you an edge over others. You can do the work, and for a fraction of the price. You can’t lose, right? WRONG! Know the value of the work that you do. If you are charging bargain basement rates, guess who you are going to attract? Bargain basement clients who don’t have the money to hire someone with your actual skill set. You are just asking to find yourself in a position where the client is short on cash and can’t pay your bills. If your rates are too low, clients may also assume you aren’t the expert that you really are. Your rates should reflect the value of your work. When you are done reading this article…. Get out there and raise those rates! If you are delivering the value, the client will see it and they will be happy to pay that invoice.

Qualifying clients is equally important. Just because someone is offering you work doesn’t mean that it’s the kind of work that is good for you and your business. If something doesn’t feel right it is OK to say “no” to their offer of work. I know this is often easier said than done. If in your initial meetings with a client regarding a project you are met with confrontations or a difference of opinion on key parts of the project, you may find yourself butting heads for the next several months while you are working with them. Disagreements can easily turn in to non-payment issues. Work with clients who are going to be a good fit for you and your brand. And once you find that client get everything in writing. Being a good fit and being on the same page with your client helps avoid any disputes down the line that might result in non-payment.

Payments and Terms

One of the easiest way to avoid late and non-payment is to ask your clients to pay you upfront. Before the project even starts ask your client for payment. If your client is unwilling or unable to pay for the project upfront, I suggest offering to break the payments down in to 50/50 payments, but with an offer for a discount for payment in full upfront. Everyone loves a discount! If you simply aren’t able to offer your clients the option of upfront payments for whatever reason, use milestone payments instead. Collect payment at every milestone, so there is no way you will end up doing work that you are never paid for. I even suggest sending the milestone invoice out at least a couple weeks in advance so that your client has time to look it over and pay. This will help avoid any halt in work due to non-payment at the milestones.

If you aren’t able to collect up front and have to send out invoices as you work, make sure you are clear with your customers about your terms (and make sure they are clearly marked on the invoice!). I usually recommend giving your client no more than 21 days to pay the invoice. Any longer than that and it becomes easier for the client to forget all about it. Shorter terms keeps the invoice fresh on their minds.

Be Visible to Your Customer

This may sound silly, but how often do you talk to your clients? I don’t mean send an email update about a project or email an invoice. I mean call them on the phone, have an in person conversation (if at all possible) or a Skype meeting. As someone who does AR work for freelancers I can’t even tell you the number of times I was asked to help collect on an invoice, and the freelancer I am working with doesn’t even have a phone number for their client. Being more than just the person on the other end of an email makes you more human to your client. A client is far less likely to stiff a person they have a connection with.

Should you find yourself with a late payment on your hands continue to be visible. If a client owes you money for work there is nothing wrong with following up with your client. Sending friendly payment reminders help to ensure those invoices don’t go overlooked. And if your client STILL hasn’t paid, pick up that phone. It is way harder to ignore someone on the phone than it is to ignore an email. In my career of calling for freelancers to collect on past due invoices, I have not once been hung up on by a client. Pick up the phone. At the least you’ll get more information on WHY that invoice is past due.

Following these simple tips will help you find clients that want to pay your invoices, and keep you getting paid on time.
