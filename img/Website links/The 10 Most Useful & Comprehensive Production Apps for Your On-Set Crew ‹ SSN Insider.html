The 10 Most Useful & Comprehensive Production Apps for Your On-Set Crew

3 Comments
 09 Sep 2014
 By Diane Panosian


With the current array of apps on the market that facilitate on-set organization, paperwork may be a thing of the past. Gone are the days when tools and clipboards weighed down cargo pants, having now been replaced with smartphones. While iPhones still outpace Android devices when it comes to powerful production apps, the gap is swiftly closing.

Below, SSN has filtered through hundreds of apps to find the most powerful, comprehensive, and streamlined ones to make your on-set life easier.

Pocket AC (Android)
Winner: Most Comprehensive Camera Department App
Why Download? Depth of field calculator, digital runtime calculator, camera specs reference, film stocks reference, exposure, field of view calculator, focus chart, inset slate. and more.
SSN Insight: Shockingly, there isn’t a version of this unbelievably comprehensive app for the iPhone. Bar-none the best camera department app, its standout feature is the Camera Log, which allows you to organize camera reports, and includes T-stop [SV1] presets and ISO steps to quickly record info into the report. Best of all, when you want to add another take, just select “Use Info from Previous Shot.”

Shot Designer (iPhone & Android)
Winner: The “Camera Blocking on Steroids” App
Why Download? The only app you need to create lighting, camera, and blocking diagrams with an elegant, practical interface.
SSN Insight: Not only is it a camera diagram but a blueprint that can be animated, include storyboards, fold into an autopopulated shotlist, and most importantly sync with the members of your crew. Upgrade for $20 bucks and you can export, save, and print scenes. Its true beauty though is the intuitive, fluid interface that masks its incredible power.

Wrap Time (iPhone)
Winner: You’re So Money App
Why Download? Keep track of your time and how much money you should see on that almighty paycheck.
SSN Insight: While you may be bleary eyed after hour 12, this app will keep track of all the time you spend on-set. Simply input your rate, overtime specifications, and other job details, then note times of events like lunch, and it calculates the rest. When you finally do reach the end of the week, just email it to the accounting department as a friendly reminder to thank you financially.

Cinema Forms (iPhone)
Winner: Save a Tree App
Why Download? Tired of carrying around tons of paper? This app will digitally save all your paperwork, and then some.
SSN Insight: We live in the digital age, so why not take full advantage and ditch the print-outs? With this app you can toss the documents and archive everything in one place. Loaded with templates, you can log talent releases, shot lists, crew contracts, and other production paperwork, and as a bonus add-on, you can use it like a notebook for continuity, camera reports, shotlists, and more. So shell out the $30 and save a tree.

DSLR Filmmaker Toolkit (iPhone)
Winner: Most Comprehensive Cinematography App
Why Download? Features include a slate, shot log, viewfinder, depth-of-field calculator, sunrise/sunset tables, and a leveler.
SSN Insight: Don’t let the name fool you, this comprehensive app can be leveraged for much more than a DSLR camera. Yes, you can find these features elsewhere, but this toolkit wraps it up in a beautiful bow with crisp, clean lines, and textures like traces of chalk on the slate. You know it’s for pros because instead of a clapping slate animation, it displays meta-data and flashes the screen with a sound.

Camera Order (iPhone)
Winner: Sexiest Gearhead App
Why Download? Compile the gear you need from all rental houses in one place.
SSN Insight: This app is the all-inclusive reference tool for almost every conceivable piece of gear. Not only does it make one list to rule them all, but lets you cross-reference, too, and includes specifics for each piece of gear, such as weight, camera resolution, and lens aperture speeds. On top of everything else, it looks downright sleek and sexy.


Shot Lister (iPhone)
Winner: Ultimate Schedule Wizard
Why Download? A pro app for scheduling and managing shoots.
SSN Insight: The makers behind the app claim it’s the “only professional shot listing app.” That’s big talk, but it’s backed up by an infographic-like framework that clearly lays out shots. But its real treasure lies in its ability to be shared amongst crew members live. It allows shots to be checked off, and lists handy info like call and lunch times, and page count stats. Not only does it help blockbuster shoots, it’s invaluable to the one-man-band filmmaker.

Scripts Pro (iPhone)
Winner: Best Writer’s Monkey
Why Download? The number one best-selling screenwriting app makes it easy to edit scripts on the go.
SSN Insight: Are you writing your film’s third act while it’s shooting? If so, A) good luck, and B) download this app. It’s number one for a reason—it makes life easier whenever you need to write, review, or edit your script, from wherever you are. It supports Final Draft, Celtx, and PDF formats and allows for iCloud syncing so you can easily flow between devices. When all is said and done, you can export the finished script to Dropbox or email.

OnSet (iPhone)
Winner: Best Production-to-Post Streamliner
Why Download? Watch dailies with all the integral information on-screen.
SSN Insight: With OnSet you can compile, review, and aggregate clips, camera data, and script data into digestible form. Okay, so it doesn’t do any H.264 encoding, but if you’re a director, cinematographer, or producer, you can select good takes and slide that info straight to the post house. Not simply a media viewer, this commanding app pairs each daily with docs like call sheets, camera reports, and scripts in a handy side column. In addition, you can do quick-edits and sort according to scene numbers.

theGripApp (iPhone)
Winner: The Dictionary-on-the-Go App
Why Download? A glossary with pictures of everything from rigs to dollies.
SSN Insight: Are you intimidated by the mass of equipment on set and the technical lingo? Just use this cheat sheet, which takes dictionary app Gobo one step further by organizing the terms used by grip and electrical departments into four categories: dollies, cranes, hardware, and rigs. But this all-encompassing manual is not just for grips, but for cinematographers and camera assistants, too, complete with pictures and diagrams to fill in the gaps of production knowledge. If you’re looking for an Android comparable app, try the Android Grip.
