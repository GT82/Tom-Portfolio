WHAT'S THE POINT OF LIVING AVERAGE?

"Down-to-earth advice on how to become successful in life"

10 Success Tips From Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson

April 22, 2013 by Amtej Dosanjh Leave a Comment

Photo courtesy of experiencefilm.com

1. Realize you are at the controls. You push the buttons which creates output. Push the wrong button and you will be dealt with failure, push the right button and you will be rewarded success.

You have to be focused and you have to be willing to do whatever it takes, like a true warrior. You have to be willing to fight for your life. Treat every endeavor as if it’s the test of your life.

“Success at anything will always come down to this: focus & effort. And we control both.”

2. Focus on working hard and being consistent. Everything else will follow.

“Success isn’t always about ‘Greatness’, it’s about consistency. Consistent, hard work gains success. Greatness will come.”

3. Make a commitment to make a major change and take massive action to bring about that change.

“In 1995 I had $7 bucks in my pocket and knew two things: I’m broke as hell and one day I won’t be.”

4. Be bigger than life.

“Be the person that when your feet touch the floor in the morning the devil says, “aww shit.. they’re up.”

5. Where do you see yourself five years from now?

“Think back 5 yrs ago. Think of where you’re at today. Think ahead 5 yrs and what you want to accomplish. Be Unstoppable.”

6. Success is all about adapting. When you fail, reassess the situation and try again.

“If something stands between you and your success – move it. Never be denied.”

7. Don’t focus on climbing the huge mountain at once; instead focus on taking the perfect baby step.

“Success isn’t overnight. It’s when everyday you get a little better than the day before. It all adds up.”

8. Your past does not predict your future.

“I was brought up as an only child, and we were very close. But when I was 14, we got evicted. We came home to a padlock, and I looked up at my mom and she was crying, and there was nothing to do.”

9. Don’t wait for “it” instead go towards”it”

“When you walk up to opportunities door, don’t knock it…kick that bitch in, smile and introduce yourself.”

10. Be great in all areas of your life. Possess the hunger to succeed, no matter what you are doing.

“Training for me is a metaphor for life, period. The dedication, the determination, the desire, the work ethic, the great successes and the great failures – I take that into life.”
