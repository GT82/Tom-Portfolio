BADMOUTHBABY.COM

CONVERSATION

By talking to Francois Arbour of BadMouthBaby.com, we learned that visitors were having a hard time getting the products to load - they were taking more than 5 seconds to display.

PLAN

We had to get the products to load quickly; 5 seconds is an eternity when you are on the Internet. Waiting this long will cause customers to abandon the site which would send search engine rankings plummeting.

PRODUCE

We implemented a robust caching scheme starting with the slowest parts of the site: product images, product listing, and dropdown choices for options like size, color and style. All of these require different strategies to implement and maintain but they add up to the same result: raw speed.

DELIVER

In a big win for BadMouthBaby, we managed to turn the site from slow and sluggish to quick and responsive in a matter of days - page load times dropped to under 50ms.

RESULTS

“This looks really good, it’s exactly what we were looking for.” -Francois

SEE OUR WORK IN ACTION

In a big win for our client, BadMouthBaby, we managed to vastly improve their page loading times. Decreasing page load times reduces website bounce rates and boosts search engine rankings.

BOOKBROWSR.COM

CONVERSATION

Bookbrowsr approached us in August 2012 looking for help with getting their website launched. The site suffered from severe speed issues for new users.

PLAN

Our plan had three main goals:
1) Speed up the website (which was, on average, taking 15 seconds to load each page).
2) Develop a system that would allow authors to be able to feature books they’ve written alongside user recommendations.
3) Create an Ebook download system that allow authors to promote their books by offering a number of free downloads, complete with a reader feedback system.

PRODUCE

We created a brand new user experience from the ground up. By rewriting the engine that generates recommendations, cleaning up existing code and implementing a new Ebook download system, we successfully revamped Bookbrowsr into a working solution.

DELIVER

Bookbrowsr was delighted with our work every step of the way. We are proud to have brought their website out of pre-launch status to a live audience of readers and authors. They are thrilled to have their site in production, serving authors and readers all over the world and generating revenue.

RESULTS

"We love it, we love it. You really hit the nail on the head: it’s exactly what we were looking for" -Bookbrowsr

SEE OUR WORK IN ACTION

We improved Bookbrowsr's page loading speed, developed an Ebook download system and created a recommendations engine from authors to their readership.

SPRING DIAL

CONVERSATION

We were approached by Derrick Jonzun, an ex-radio radio DJ who wanted a way to make phone calls to his listeners in an automated fashion featuring either his voice or recorded media.

PLAN

We needed a robust solution that did two main things:
1) Allowed for our client to easily operate the software from a friendly interface.
2) Allowed for a wide variety of settings and functions to be completed for reaching out to radio listeners.

PRODUCE

We were able to create a ground up build for Derrick that exceeded his expectations. From the simple interface to the robust operations, Spring Dial was a complete success for our client.

DELIVER

Spring Dial is launched in beta with a popular sports talk show out of Atlanta, GA which is doing a weekly wakeup call. A number of additional radio stations have also expressed interest in the software.

RESULTS

"This is exactly how we envisioned it working." –Spring Dial

Spring Dial has positively impacted how radio stations connect to listeners. By creating a friendly interface and a high-functioning automated calling system, stations can vastly improve their approach to promotions.

LIVETELL

CONVERSATION

We were approached by LiveTell in September of 2012 when they were looking for experienced Ruby on Rails developers to augment their team. The project in question was a large legacy Rails 2.3 application that lacked test coverage and needed new features due to evolving business requirements. The system in question also needed updates to a newer version of Ruby on Rails, as well as extensive data validation and migration.

PLAN

Due to the legacy nature of the application, the first order of business was to apply as many high-level tests as possible to maintain existing features. Then, we could upgrade the entire codebase to support the latest release of Ruby on Rails.

PRODUCE

Over the course of the following year, we successfully rewrote a large portion of a business-critical portion of the application, all while maintaining the existing functionality and occasionally even adding features to the existing system.

DELIVER

Our extensive rewrite has been successfully deployed to production and our work has been live since September of 2013.

RESULTS

“Great job on the feature. I love the attention to detail.” -LiveTell

Our extensive update and test coverage improvements allowed for existing business-critical applications to maintain their utility, while adding additional functionality and new features.
